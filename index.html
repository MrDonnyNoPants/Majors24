1 What Is the Majors 24?

The Majors 24 is a 24-hour endurance sim race run on iRacing, modeled after the real-world 24 Hours of Le Mans. It is organised by the Majors Series — a league dedicated to replicating the traditions and prestige of the world's greatest motorsport events inside the simulator.

Unlike a typical 30-minute sprint race, endurance racing is a completely different animal. The goal is not just to go fast — it's to go fast consistently, manage a car over many hours, hand off between teammates smoothly, and make as few mistakes as possible over an entire day and night cycle.

New to Endurance Racing? Read This First The #1 mindset shift: finishing beats everything. A clean lap that is 2 seconds slower than your max is worth far more than a fast lap that ends in a wall. Pace yourself, protect the car, and keep it on track. Points, positions, and glory come from surviving.

The event takes place at Circuit de la Sarthe (Le Mans) in iRacing, running for a full simulated 24 hours. Our team is entered in the GT3 class — the most popular and accessible class in the field, competing on track alongside faster LMP2 Prototypes and GTP Hypercars.

4 Pre-Qualifying (PQ) — Your First Big Task

Pre-Qualifying determines which "split" your team races in. It's a seeding process — faster teams get placed with others at their level. Every split races the full 24 hours.

How PQ Works

  • Each session is a 1-hour solo qualifying session with a "best of 4" format — you have up to 4 laps to set your best time.
  • Sessions run May 30 – June 5, 8 times per day every 3 hours (see Section 2 for times in your timezone).
  • You do not need to stay the full hour — set your time and leave.
  • Drivers do not need to be in the same session. Each person qualifies on their own schedule.

How the Team Average Is Calculated

The Majors Series takes your three fastest drivers' times and averages them. This average determines your split. All other drivers still need to complete PQ to be eligible to race — their times just don't affect split placement.

⚠ Strategy: Who Are Your Top 3? The three drivers with the best PQ times become your "nominated drivers," marked GREEN on the results list. These three must race — a no-show from any of them costs a 1-lap post-race penalty.

PQ Tips for GT3

  • Free qualifying setups for every GT3 car are in the Majors Garage on Discord.
  • PQ conditions are different from race conditions — the track is less rubbered in. Don't be surprised if the car feels different on race day.
  • There's no time-of-day advantage to when you qualify — pick sessions that fit your schedule.
  • Don't sandbag (intentionally slow). If race officials find your team sandbagged to reach a lower split, you face lap penalties.

5 Splits Explained

A "split" is a separate race instance. Because hundreds of teams enter, the field is divided into groups of roughly 54 cars each. Split 1 has the fastest teams; higher-numbered splits are progressively slower. Every split runs the full 24-hour race.

  • Split placement is based on your team's average PQ time.
  • Your time must be within 104% of your class pole-sitter's time, or you're bumped to the next split.
  • The split password is emailed to the Team Contact Person after PQ closes, and is also on the Majors website's PQ results page. You need this to enter the race server.
  • Splits 1–4 have Safety Car periods. Splits 5–6 have one each. Splits 7+ have none.
ℹ Note for New Teams Don't stress about which split you land in — your competition will be at a similar level. Every split is a legitimate race with its own winner. Focus on running clean and finishing well.

6 Your Car: GT3 Class

GT3 is the most popular and accessible class in the Majors 24. These are production-based GT race cars — Ferrari 296 GT3, Porsche 992 GT3 R, BMW M4 GT3, Mercedes AMG GT3, Aston Martin Vantage GT3, and others. They're slower than LMP2 and GTP but more forgiving, and perfect for endurance racing.

Key Facts

  • Open setup event — use any setup you like. Free setups for every GT3 car are in the Majors Garage on Discord.
  • You can switch to any GT3 car at any time during the race — even mid-race after a pitstop. No declaration needed.
  • Different teammates can run different GT3 cars within the same team (though sticking to one car is simpler logistically).
  • Race-day weather is auto-generated by iRacing and will differ from PQ conditions.
ℹ Multi-Class Reminder You will share the circuit with GTP Hypercars and LMP2 Prototypes — both significantly faster. This is completely normal. See Section 11 for how to handle this correctly.

7 Driver Stints & Fair Share

Fair Share is ON. Each driver must complete a minimum number of laps or the entry is penalised. iRacing enforces this automatically.

The Formula

How Fair Share Is Calculated

Step 1 — Equal Share: Total race laps ÷ number of drivers (rounded down)

Step 2 — Fair Share Minimum: Equal Share ÷ 4 (rounded down)

Each driver must complete at least this many laps.

Example with 6 drivers and ~360 laps total:
Equal Share = 360 ÷ 6 = 60 laps · Fair Share Minimum = 60 ÷ 4 = 15 laps per driver (~60 min)

Estimated Minimums — GT3 Class (~4 min/lap)

Team SizeEqual Share (est.)Fair Share Minimum (est.)Time at ~4 min/lap
6 drivers~60 laps~15 laps~60 minutes
7 drivers~51 laps~12 laps~48 minutes
8 drivers~45 laps~11 laps~44 minutes
⚠ These Are Minimums, Not Targets The Fair Share minimum is just a floor — everyone needs a real stint. With 8 drivers doing 11 laps each that's only 88 laps; you need the car running for ~360. Plan stints so someone is always driving.

Stint Planning Tips

  • Plan the rotation before race day. Assign time slots to each driver in advance. Know who drives when and who's on standby.
  • GT3 tyre stints are typically 30–60 minutes depending on pace. Plan driver changes around pitstops naturally.
  • Brief the incoming driver on track conditions, any damage, and traffic before handing over.
  • Keep a shared group chat note tracking who has driven and how many laps — so no one accidentally misses their Fair Share minimum.
  • Your 3 nominated drivers (Top 3 in PQ) must race. Don't accidentally skip one.
✅ Suggested Approach for 6–8 Drivers With 6 drivers, aim for ~2–3 hour stints each. With 8 drivers, ~2 hours each works well. Build a small buffer above the minimum so a late-race incident can't knock someone below Fair Share.

9 Grid Photo & Hyperpole

Grid Photo

The grid photo is a Le Mans tradition that every split recreates. It's optional but highly encouraged — great team keepsake.

  • The qualifying driver joins immediately after the Drivers Meeting ends.
  • Lap the track to the start/finish straight. Do not drive backwards from pit exit — this can trigger a sim disqualification.
  • GT3 cars line up at the back (GTP first, LMP2 behind them, GT3 last).
  • Take screenshots once most of the field is assembled. You have until the bottom of the hour before Hyperpole.
  • Share photos on the Majors Discord!

Hyperpole (Grid Qualifying)

A lone 4-lap session run 30 minutes before the race. Your fastest lap sets your starting position within the GT3 class.

  • Only one driver qualifies the car. They do not have to be the race starter.
  • Weather is auto-generated; track state starts just above 0%.
  • After Hyperpole, the car returns to the pits and can be handed to the starting driver.

10 Race Start Procedure

The race uses a formation lap followed by a green flag. Incidents before the green are penalised more harshly than mid-race incidents.

GT3-Specific Instructions

  • During the formation lap, the GT3 pole sitter must leave a 10-second gap to the last LMP2 car ahead.
  • GT3 cars should be in single file before the final chicanes.
  • The GT3 leader goes on the green flag — react when the car ahead accelerates. This is the safest approach and avoids chain collisions.
⚠ Start Hazards — Harshly Penalised Jumping the start, weaving aggressively to warm tyres and pushing another car off, or rear-ending someone in a concertina effect are all avoidable. Be patient. It is a 24-hour race.

11 Multi-Class Etiquette & Blue Flags

This is the area where newer endurance drivers get into trouble most often. Pay close attention.

You are in GT3 — the slowest class on track. GTP Hypercars and LMP2 Prototypes lap significantly faster throughout the entire race. This is normal — as long as everyone behaves correctly.

When a Faster-Class Car Is Approaching You

✅ DO This

  • Hold your normal racing line
  • Maintain a predictable, consistent speed
  • Be aware of where they are — use your mirrors
  • Let them pass safely
  • Continue racing normally once they're by

❌ NEVER Do This

  • Block or make a defensive move against them
  • Suddenly swerve to "get out of the way"
  • Brake unexpectedly to give room
  • Change your line abruptly
  • Panic and drive off the racing line into a wall
ℹ The Golden Rule for Multi-Class Hold your line and be predictable. An unexpected swerve is far more dangerous than staying put. Fast-class cars are experienced at passing around you — let them do their job.

When Racing Another GT3 Car

  • You are allowed ONE defensive move. A second move to re-block is illegal and protestable.
  • Wheel-to-wheel GT3 racing is encouraged — just keep it clean.

Blue Flags — What They Mean Here

Blue flags in the Majors 24 are not like F1. You do not have to immediately pull over.

SituationYour Obligation
A faster-class car is approaching you Do NOT block or impede. You do NOT have to move off the racing line — just stay predictable.
A same-class car more than 1 lap ahead is approaching Do NOT defend or unnaturally impede. Do NOT have to move off the racing line.
Racing your class leader to stay on the lead lap You CAN race hard to stay on the lead lap — this is allowed. Still follow normal driving standards.
You are genuinely faster than a car laps ahead of you You can overtake. Once past, pull away — don't then be slower than them and impede them again.

12 Safety Car (SC) Procedure

The SC procedure is complex and specific to this event — don't assume it works like any other series.

⚠ SC Only Applies to Splits 1–6 Splits 7 and up have no Safety Car periods at all. If you're in a higher split, this section may not apply to you.

When Is the SC Deployed?

The SC is triggered at a random, pre-determined time by Race Control — not by on-track incidents. It will not occur in the first or last 30 minutes of the race.

When SC Is Called — Step by Step

1
Race Control announces: "SAFETY CAR, SAFETY CAR, SAFETY CAR" verbally and in text chat. Listen for it.
2
Keep racing speed. Do NOT pass other GT3 cars from this moment on — even lapped ones. You CAN pass other classes (GTP, LMP2) until you reach the SC queue.
3
Catch the queue at racing speed. Close the gap to the car ahead. Once in the SC queue: NO passing anyone at all, regardless of class.
4
Pit lane entry is always open. However, the pit exit closes each time the SC queue passes it. If you come out when the exit is closed, stop at the blue cone and wait. You rejoin at the back of the queue.
5
Wait for wave-by instructions from Race Control. Only move forward if told to. Taking a wave-by without direction is penalised.
6
Restart zone: final chicane. No overtaking before the start/finish line. Zero overlapping before the apex of the final corner. The leader chooses when to accelerate — follow only once they go.

13 Spare Car & Black Flags

Spare Car / Fast Repair

Each team gets one fast repair during the race. Save it for a significant incident — minor damage can be repaired in a normal pitstop.

Black Flags

⚠ Critical: Black Flag Rules If you receive a black flag: serve it during normal racing, not under the Safety Car. If you get a black flag during an SC period, immediately tell Race Control — they will clear it and re-issue it after the restart.
  • If you serve a black flag under the SC without notifying Race Control: 1-lap post-race penalty.
  • If you ignore a black flag and get auto-DSQ'd by iRacing for crossing the start line too many times: Race Control cannot help — your race is done.

14 Protests & Race Control

Race Control

Live Race Control is active in most splits throughout the race. They can issue penalties before a protest is even filed. Their decisions are final during the race.

Protest Tokens

Each team starts with 2 protest tokens. If your protest is upheld, the token is returned. If not, you lose it permanently. When all tokens are gone, no more protests can be filed.

When to File a Protest

✅ File when:

  • Your team was directly involved
  • There was clear, measurable harm
  • The other driver made an obvious significant error

❌ Don't file when:

  • You weren't involved in the incident
  • It was a racing incident with no clear fault
  • Frivolous protests can result in a penalty for you

File at: majorsseries.com/mec_protests

15 Code of Conduct

  • Keep all chat civil — profanity or offensive chat toward other drivers can result in a penalty.
  • No naming and shaming on the Majors Discord. File a protest instead.
  • Accept decisions gracefully — publicly attacking protest decisions on Discord is a conduct violation.
  • Zero retaliation — intentional payback incidents result in immediate disqualification. No exceptions.
  • Keep the radio clear for race-critical communication. Use private channels for team comms.
